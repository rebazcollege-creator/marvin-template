Biography

Between the page and the stage

Ardalan Bastani is a Kurdish writer, playwright and theatre-maker from Bukan, a city in the Kurdistan region of Iran (Rojhelat). His work grows out of the experience of exile and resistance — a body of writing and performance that carries the Kurdish voice across borders and languages.

Active in the student movement in Iran, he left the country in 2014 and arrived in Germany in 2015. Since 2017 he has lived in Berlin, where he is part of the city's independent cultural and political life. He writes in Kurdish and Persian, performs in German, and works as a translator between them.

His path runs through theatre stages, the pages of published books, and the columns of Kurdish magazines — always returning to the same questions: home and displacement, freedom, memory, and the worlds we carry with us when we leave one place for another.

On Stage

Theatre & Performance

Arriving in Germany as a refugee, Ardalan turned to the stage as a place to speak. He helped build theatre out of shelters and collectives — as actor, translator and maker.

  1. Letter TO THE WORLD

    Initiator · Actor · Translator · Organiser

    A theatre project he initiated together with other refugees from his emergency shelter — turning a shared experience of displacement into performance.

  2. Deine Heimat. Meine Heimat

    Translator · Assistant Director

    A follow-up production exploring belonging and homeland, on which he worked behind the scenes as translator and assistant director.

  3. Freiheit · Alles | was wir haben · Offen lag die Welt

    Actor

    Stage productions in Berlin's independent theatre scene in which he appeared as a performer.

Words & Voice

Writing & Journalism

Editor — Tîjhang magazine

From 2016 to 2019 he served as editor of the Kurdish magazine Tîjhang, published by the Manjanîgh collective — a platform for Kurdish writing in the diaspora.

Essays & commentary

He writes and speaks on Kurdistan, exile and the politics of the region — including on the women-led uprising in Iran and the Kurdish question.

Across three languages

Writing in Kurdish and Persian and working in German, his practice is shaped by translation — carrying ideas and stories between cultures.
